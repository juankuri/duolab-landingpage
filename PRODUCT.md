# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary: Carla, 34, mother of two (preschool + primary school age), Ciudad del Carmen, Campeche. Researches via WhatsApp mom-groups, Facebook, then Google Maps reviews. Arrives with concrete doubts — does she need an appointment, what will it cost, will the staff know how to handle a child who's scared of needles. Decision is emotional and risk-averse: she's protecting her kids.

Secondary: Roberto, 42, doesn't seek healthcare voluntarily — comes because a doctor ordered studies or his partner pushed him. Wants to "get it over with": no questions, no waiting, clear price up front, fast in-and-out.

Both find the lab through Google Maps / WhatsApp recommendation, decide largely on reviews + price clarity, and convert via a WhatsApp message before showing up.

## Product Purpose

A single-page conversion landing for a clinical analysis lab. It exists to turn a doubtful searcher into a WhatsApp conversation ("Hablar con un especialista") by resolving — in the visitor's own language, not institutional language — the handful of doubts that block the visit: appointment requirement, price uncertainty, trust in results, and (for the primary persona) confidence that children will be treated gently. Success is a visitor who reaches the final CTA already reassured, not just informed.

## Brand Personality

Cercano (warm, not institutional) · Claro (no jargon, rewrite anything a patient wouldn't understand) · Profesional (expertise without arrogance) · Tranquilizador (the visitor arrives anxious; the brand meets that with calm, not urgency).

Voice examples from the client's own brand guide: say "Más de 50 análisis disponibles. Sin cita. Sin sorpresas de precio," not "Realizamos estudios de diagnóstico clínico de alta complejidad." Say "Si tu hijo le tiene miedo a las agujas, aquí sabemos cómo tratarlo," not language naming a medical specialty for child care.

## Anti-references

- Cold, institutional hospital-site language — anything that reads like a form letter rather than someone trustworthy talking to you.
- Medical jargon the average patient wouldn't understand; if a word needs explaining, it gets rewritten instead.
- Any mention of insurance or IMSS arrangements (the lab has none) and the word "Contáctenos."
- Generic AI-template SaaS landing pages: purple-to-blue gradient heroes, gradient text, identical icon-card grids, hero-metric blocks, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows, numbered section markers used as default scaffolding, glassmorphism-as-decoration. The brand's own purple (#6B4A8E) is real and earned — it must not end up looking like a SaaS template that happens to be purple.
- Stock-photo sterility — favor real, specific signals (years in operation, study count, turnaround time) over generic trust iconography.

## Design Principles

- Resolve doubts in the visitor's own words, not the lab's. Every section answers a question the persona actually asks ("¿necesito cita?", "¿cuánto cuesta?", "¿son buenos con niños?") rather than describing services abstractly.
- Reassurance over urgency. The visitor already arrived anxious; the page should calm, not pressure.
- One differentiator gets its own visual weight: gentle, experienced handling of children is the strongest decision driver for the primary persona and must read as a distinct, prominent section — not a bullet buried in a list.
- Quantitative trust over generic testimonials. Until real reviews are sourced, trust is carried by concrete numbers (years of operation, number of studies, turnaround time), never placeholder quotes.
- Mobile-first, single clear path to one action: every section reinforces the same WhatsApp CTA, never introduces a competing goal.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Mobile-first; must be fully usable and legible on a mid-range phone without zooming or horizontal scrolling.
- Plain language by default — short sentences, no medical jargon, "tú" form throughout (per spec FR-009).
- Sufficient color contrast against the brand's purple/lilac palette (body text must not wash out against tinted backgrounds).
- The children-focused section must stay clearly delimited so visitors without children don't feel excluded or that the page wasn't meant for them.
